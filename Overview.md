# The Celebrations Budget — One‑Page Overview
### Underlying Framework: Occasions Budgeting Method (OBM)

**The Celebrations Budget** is a structured, intentional approach to drinking across the year.  
It reframes alcohol not as a routine behaviour, but as something reserved for meaningful, pre‑selected moments — **holidays, celebrations, milestones, gatherings, and events that genuinely matter**.

At its core, The Celebrations Budget is built on a simple idea:

> Not every drink is a celebration — so choose the ones that are.

To support this, the system uses the **Occasions Budgeting Method (OBM)**, a behavioural framework that allocates a fixed number of “Occasions” per year. An Occasion is a defined period — from a single evening to a multi‑day holiday — during which alcohol is permitted. By limiting the number of Occasions rather than the number of drinks, OBM shifts the focus from restriction to intention, from quantity to meaning.

OBM works through three behavioural mechanisms:

1. **Pre‑commitment**  
   Occasions are declared in advance, reducing impulsive drinking and strengthening intentionality.

2. **Scarcity**  
   A fixed annual allocation (typically 22 Occasions) encourages selectivity and reflection.

3. **Meaningful framing**  
   Drinking becomes tied to significance — holidays, celebrations, and important moments — rather than habit.

The Celebrations Budget is the public‑facing expression of this system.  
OBM is the structural backbone that makes it work.

This repository exists to timestamp and establish authorship of the method, its terminology, and its conceptual framing. Full documentation is held privately by the author.
