## The Celebrations Budget
**The Celebrations Budget** is the public‑facing expression of this framework — a structured, intentional way to choose the moments in a year when alcohol is part of a celebration. It reframes alcohol not as a routine behaviour, but as something reserved for meaningful, pre‑selected moments — **holidays, celebrations, milestones, gatherings, and events that genuinely matter**.

## Occasions Budgeting Method (OBM)
Created by Rob, 2026.

The Occasions Budgeting Method (OBM) is the behavioural system that underpins The Celebrations Budget. OBM uses a fixed annual allocation of “Occasions” to regulate alcohol consumption through pre‑commitment, scarcity, and intentional planning. An Occasion is defined as a pre‑declared period — from a single event to a multi‑day holiday — during which alcohol use is permitted under a fixed annual allocation.

OBM works through three behavioural mechanisms:

- **Pre‑commitment** — Occasions are declared in advance, reducing impulsive drinking.  
- **Scarcity** — A fixed annual allocation (typically 22 Occasions) encourages selectivity.  
- **Meaningful framing** — Drinking becomes tied to significance rather than habit.

OBM provides the structural backbone for The Celebrations Budget, ensuring that celebratory drinking is meaningful, deliberate, and aligned with personal values.
